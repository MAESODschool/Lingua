PART 2A — Regular verbs rule 1
Rule 1:
General verbs add -ed
Examples:
walk → walked
jump → jumped
wash → washed
look → looked
open → opened
After teaching this rule, give a short practice drill immediately.
Do NOT wait until the end.
Then after practice, fight a weak small enemy.
Small practice enemy themes should be related to time / past / rewind / memory.
Examples of small enemies:
Time Dust
Echo Tick
Yesterday Sprite
Rewind Slime
These are light practice enemies, not bosses.
PART 2B — Regular verbs rule 2
Rule 2:
Verbs ending with e add only -d
Examples:
like → liked
love → loved
live → lived
dance → danced
close → closed
Teach first.
Then short practice.
Then a light enemy battle or mini drill.
PART 2C — Regular verbs rule 3
Rule 3:
Verbs ending with y
Case A:
If there is a vowel before y, add -ed normally
play → played
enjoy → enjoyed
stay → stayed
Case B:
If there is a consonant before y, change y to i and add -ed
study → studied
cry → cried
try → tried
carry → carried
Teach first.
Then short practice.
Then light enemy battle or mini drill.
PART 2D — Regular verbs rule 4
Rule 4:
Short CVC verbs double the final consonant before adding -ed
Examples:
stop → stopped
plan → planned
drop → dropped
clap → clapped
Teach first.
Then short practice.
Then light enemy battle or mini drill.
Make the rule label clear:
“CVC = พยัญชนะ + สระ + พยัญชนะ”
Students should be able to see the categories clearly:
เติม -ed
ลงท้าย e เติม -d
ลงท้าย y
CVC doubled consonant

8. Battle system changes
The battle system is okay overall, but please change the parry system.
8.1 Warning timing is too fast
Slow it down.
When the enemy is about to attack:
show a warning first
show a button for the student to press:
“เตรียมพร้อม”
only after the student presses that button should the parry interface begin
This gives the player time to prepare mentally.
8.2 Replace current parry system
Do NOT use countdown anymore.
Create a new parry mechanic like this:
There is a parry bar
A green target zone moves
A white line / marker also moves quickly across the bar
The player must tap at the right moment so the white line lands inside the green zone
The green zone becomes smaller over time
If the player reacts too slowly, it becomes harder
This should create a more skill-based timing system.
Important:
the green zone must move
the white line must move quickly
the green zone shrinks gradually over time
the player must tap to stop / trigger the white line at the correct moment
This new parry system replaces the old countdown-based parry.

Requirement:

Examples:

If it is the enemy’s turn, enlarge or highlight the enemy
This should help students immediately know whose turn it is.
